Our Ethos

Cuisine as
Conversation

At NOIR, we reject the ordinary. Every plate is a dialogue between nature and craft — between what grows in the earth and what the hands of our team can coax it to become.

We source exclusively from small farms, independent fishermen, and artisan producers who share our obsession with provenance. Nothing here is accidental.

01
The Origin

Born from a
Single Vision

NOIR began in 2019 when chef Élise Marchand asked a single question: what if a restaurant felt like someone had designed it just for you? No compromise, no convention — only the relentless pursuit of a meal that stays with you for years.

02
The Philosophy

Season First,
Menu Second

Our menu does not dictate the kitchen. The land does. Every Monday morning, chef Élise reviews what has arrived from our network of 34 farmers across the northeast. Only then does the week's menu take shape.

03
The Recognition

Three Stars.
One Standard.

In 2022, NOIR was awarded three Michelin stars — the third earned in under three years of opening. The guide called it "a restaurant of total conviction." We keep our focus not on accolades, but on the next service.

01
Provenance Over Everything

We work directly with 34 farms, three fishing families, and two specialist foragers. We know every supplier by name and visit them regularly.

02
The Menu Follows the Market

Our menu is never fixed. Every week it changes based on what has arrived. We do not force ingredients into dishes — we let the ingredients define them.

03
Waste is Failure

Every part of every ingredient is used — stems become stocks, trim becomes sauce, shells become bisques. Sustainability is not a position. It is how we cook.

04
The Kitchen Grows People

Eleven alumni have gone on to open their own restaurants, three of which now hold Michelin stars. We take this as our proudest achievement.
